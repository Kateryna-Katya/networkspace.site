About the Game

ROOTED IN
ANCIENT
FIRE

Triple Chili draws inspiration from Mayan and Aztec mythology — cultures that understood the real power behind their symbols and ceremonies.The visual language of the game is shaped by elements brought from the New World: peppers wrapped in flame, corn, fruits, and stone carvings that somehow feel both ancient and alive.

At the center of this world stands the pyramid — a symbol of balance and dominance.
It acts as a force that adapts, fills gaps, and reshapes outcomes when it appears.
What we ended up with? A game that actually feels substantial. It's got this warm, almost hypnotic quality that keeps pulling you back in.

Game Structure

Built for Presence,
Not Rush

So we did 5 reels by 8 rows because we wanted space to breathe.
You know how some games feel like they're trying to cram everything into a tiny box? Yeah, we hate that too.
With up to 60 ways to win, each round has room to actually build up naturally.
Nothing feels cramped. Nothing feels random. The board breathes — and so does the player.
Subtle fire and sound effects accompany each action, reinforcing the sense of ritual rather than distraction.

Wild Pyramid

The Pyramid
Symbol

The pyramid marked with the Wild inscription is the most dominant element in the game.
It can replace any standard symbol to complete meaningful combinations and often becomes the turning point of a round.
The Wild pyramid needs to feel like it weighs a ton - like some ancient stone monument that's been there forever, not just another shiny gimmick we slapped on. This symbol is all about control, not random mayhem.

Atmosphere & Experience

A Controlled
Flame

Every detail in Triple Chili is designed to maintain a steady intensity.
The sound design leans into rhythm and low percussion.
Animations are fiery but restrained — glowing embers, slow flame movement, warm highlights. If you're tired of games that are all flash and no substance, this might be your thing. We actually put some thought into this one.
